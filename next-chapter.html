Graduation is an ending and a beginning.

The real journey starts now. This is my roadmap for the next five years: where I'm going, how I'll get there, and who I want to become. Not a rigid plan, but a direction. Not a guarantee, but a commitment.

The Immediate Next: 90 Days

What happens between now and three months from graduation.

Weeks 0-4

Search & Apply

Job applications go out today. Not waiting for the perfect role. Perfect doesn't exist. I'm targeting: food tech companies, research labs, quality assurance roles. Minimum 20 applications per week. Informational interviews with people already inside.

  • Update LinkedIn profile
  • CV finalized and reviewed
  • 20+ applications sent
  • 5+ informational interviews scheduled
Priority: High

Weeks 5-12

Interview & Decide

Second wave of applications. Interviews start coming in. I'm looking for: company culture fit, technical challenge, growth opportunity, mentorship access. Not just a job—a launch point.

  • Interview prep (technical + behavioral)
  • Attend all interviews scheduled
  • Get multiple offers (goal: 3+)
  • Evaluate culture + tech stack
Priority: Critical

Week 12

Commit & Onboard

Decision made. Contract signed. First onboarding meeting scheduled. I'm not trying to be perfect on day one. I'm trying to be ready to learn. Setup laptop, meet the team, understand the codebase/processes—that's week one.

  • Choose role / sign contract
  • Notify other companies (professionally)
  • Prepare first-day notes
  • Mental prep for new chapter
Priority: Execution

Five-Year Career Pathway

Phase 1

Months 0-6: Entry & Learning

Year 1 Q1-Q2

Focus: Master fundamentals. Learn company culture, processes, team dynamics.

Goals

  • Complete onboarding successfully
  • Ship first feature (even if small)
  • Get to know team deeply
  • Build 1:1 with mentor

Phase 2

Months 6-18: Establish & Contribute

Year 1 Q3-4 + Year 2 Q1

Focus: Build credibility. Take ownership of projects. Become go-to person for specific domain.

Goals

  • Own 1-2 significant projects
  • Become expert in specific area
  • Lead code reviews / help juniors
  • First performance review (exceeds expectations)

Phase 3

Months 18-36: Lead & Grow

Year 2 Q2-Q4 + Year 3 Q1

Focus: Take leadership role. Mentor others. Contribute to company strategy, not just execution.

Goals

  • Lead cross-functional project
  • Mentor junior team members
  • Contribute to hiring/culture
  • First promotion or title progression

Phase 4

Months 36-48: Specialize & Decide

Year 3 Q2-Q4

Focus: Deep specialization or leadership track. Decide career direction: technical or managerial.

Goals

  • Become recognized expert in field
  • Lead team or drive major initiative
  • Published/shared knowledge externally
  • Clear career direction for next phase

Phase 5

Months 48-60: Vision & Impact

Year 4-5

Focus: Make meaningful impact. Build something lasting. Start thinking about next leap (startup, senior role, pivot).

Goals

  • Ship significant product/feature
  • Build and lead team of 5+
  • Strategic influence on company
  • Clear vision for next chapter

The Real Talk: What Could Go Wrong

I'm not naive. Let me name the challenges and how I'll handle them.

Impostor Syndrome

The Risk: Everyone else seems to know what they're doing. I'll feel like a fraud. Doubt myself. Get frozen by perfectionism.

How I'll Handle It: Remind myself that everyone feels this way. Ask for help early. Write down my wins (no matter how small). Find a mentor who gets it. Talk to people who've been there.

Burnout & Overwhelm

The Risk: Try to do too much too fast. Skip sleep. Skip friends. Wake up burnt out by month 4.

How I'll Handle It: Set hard boundaries. "Office hours" for work (clock out at X). Schedule personal time like I schedule meetings. Check in with myself monthly. If I'm drowning, I'll speak up early.

Wrong Company / Role

The Risk: Take the first offer. Realize at month 3 it's not a fit. Team is toxic. Work is boring. Growth is limited.

How I'll Handle It: Be selective in the first place (culture check, talk to current employees). Give it 6 months before deciding. If it's genuinely wrong, I'll look for next role. It's not failure—it's learning.

Imposing Limiting Beliefs

The Risk: Tell myself I'm "not technical enough" or "not smart enough." Stay small. Don't apply for stretch roles.

How I'll Handle It: Remember what I've already done. Go back to exams I aced. Lab work I nailed. Projects I shipped. I've already proven I can do hard things.

Isolation

The Risk: Stay heads-down on work. Spend weekends alone. Lose my support system from university.

How I'll Handle It: Schedule friend time (yes, actually schedule it). Join communities—food tech meetups, hackathons, professional groups. Be intentional about building community.

Comparing My Beginning to Their Middle

The Risk: Compare my role at month 6 to their role at year 4. Get discouraged. Think I'm falling behind.

How I'll Handle It: Unfollow/mute people who trigger comparison spiral. Focus on my own trajectory, not theirs. Celebrate small wins. Remember: everyone's timeline is different.

This Is My Commitment

I'm not waiting for permission. I'm not waiting for the perfect job. I'm not waiting until I feel ready (I probably never will).

Starting today, I commit to:

  • Show up. Every day. On time. Prepared. Even when it's hard.
  • Learn aggressively. Ask questions. Read code. Understand systems. Never assume I know enough.
  • Build relationships. The work matters. The people matter more.
  • Stay humble. I don't have all the answers. That's fine. That's where growth happens.
  • Take ownership. If something's broken, I'll fix it. If something's unclear, I'll clarify it.
  • Keep growing. Technical skills, leadership skills, emotional intelligence. Never stop pushing.
  • Stay authentic. Don't pretend to be someone I'm not. The right fit will see me and appreciate me.
  • Give myself grace. I'll make mistakes. That's not failure. That's learning.

This is my next chapter. I'm ready to write it.
