Deep Dive · Part 01

Where I Come From

Hometown, childhood, family rhythms, and the quiet moments that built my foundation.

Before UJ, before ambition took shape, there was home. This is the story of the place, the people, and the small routines that taught me who I was before I knew who I wanted to become.

The beginning of everything.

My Hometown

A Place of Roots and Work Ethic

My hometown is more than coordinates on a map. It's the rhythm of early mornings, neighbors who knew my name, and a community that held each other to high standards.

Growing up here, I learned that hard work was not negotiable. It was the currency we all used. Whether it was school, chores, or responsibilities, there was no room for shortcuts. People here believed in building things that lasted.

I remember my parents' generation talking about opportunity like it was a precious thing. Not something to take for granted. That shaped me more than I realized at the time.

What I Learned Here

  • Consistency beats intensity
  • Community matters more than comfort
  • Respect is earned, not given
  • Opportunity is a responsibility
  • Small things build into big things
The Spaces That Built Me

Early Childhood: Curiosity & Routine

I was always asking "why?"

The Kid Who Asked Why

I was curious about everything. How things worked. Why people made the choices they did. Why some days felt easier than others.

My parents say I would follow them around the kitchen, the garage, anywhere they were working, just asking endless questions. They didn't shut me down. They explained things. They let me watch. They let me try.

That early permission to be curious shaped me more than any formal lesson could have. It taught me that asking for understanding was okay. That being interested in how things worked was valuable.

Age 5-7

Always in the kitchen, watching, learning rhythms of cooking and preparation.

Age 7-10

Started understanding cause and effect. Why recipes worked. Why consistency mattered.

Age 10-12

Helping more deliberately. Taking on responsibility. Understanding that care and precision made a difference.

Moments That Matter

Family: The Quiet Foundation

I grew up in a household where stability was not loud. It was consistent. My parents didn't (and still don't) make big gestures. They make small, steady ones. Every single day.

MOM

My Mother

The One Who Shows Me How

She is methodical. She plans. She executes. She doesn't talk about her work—she just does it, and it's done well. She taught me that excellence is not about talking about what you're going to do. It's about doing it, consistently, without needing recognition.

Lesson: Show, don't tell. Consistency over performance.

DAD

My Father

The One Who Asks Questions

He encouraged me to think. To ask why. To not accept things just because that's how they've always been. He taught me that curiosity is strength, not weakness. That understanding is worth the time it takes.

Lesson: Understanding matters. Keep asking questions.

HOME

The House

The Container of Routine

Our home wasn't fancy. It was calm. Predictable in the best way. There was rhythm to it. Breakfast at a certain time. Work. Dinner together. Conversations. Sleep. And it all repeated. That rhythm taught me that stability is built through repetition, not through lucky moments.

Lesson: Routine is your foundation. Build rituals deliberately.

Family Moments & Rhythms

The Influences That Shaped Me

It wasn't just family. There were teachers, neighbors, community members, moments—all of which planted seeds that grew into who I am today.

Teacher · Age 8

The One Who Made Learning Feel Like Play

There was a teacher who made me feel like my questions mattered. Like curiosity was the point, not a distraction. She showed me science wasn't about memorizing facts. It was about understanding how things worked.

Impact: I learned that learning could be a joy, not a chore.

Mentor · Age 12-14

The One Who Showed Me Possibility

A community member who had pursued specialized work. Seeing someone from my neighborhood succeed outside it showed me that bigger opportunities were possible. That my hometown wasn't a limit; it was a starting point.

Impact: I learned that origin doesn't determine destination.

Peer · Age 10-16

The One Who Pushed Me

A friend who competed with me, challenged me, and refused to let me settle. We pushed each other in school, in sports, in everything. That friction taught me that healthy competition makes you better.

Impact: I learned that people who raised my standards were gifts.

When Curiosity Became Ambition

12-14

Realization: School Mattered

I started understanding that what I did in school had consequences beyond school. It affected what I could become, where I could go. This wasn't about grades for grades' sake. It was about building capacity.

14-16

Focus Sharpens: Science & Process

By high school, I knew I was drawn to how things worked. I became obsessed with chemistry, physics, the logic of systems. I wanted to understand not just facts, but the why behind them.

16-18

The Dream Takes Shape: Food Technology

I discovered food technology. It combined everything: chemistry, process discipline, real-world impact, and the ability to create something people actually needed. I realized I could build a career around understanding how things worked—and have that work matter.

18

Ready: Walking Into UJ

Everything I'd learned from my hometown—work ethic, curiosity, the value of consistency, the importance of understanding—came with me. I wasn't running away from where I came from. I was bringing it with me into the next chapter.

What My Origin Made Clear

"I don't come from a place of luxury. I come from a place of responsibility. That's actually the better inheritance."

Everything I am today was built in those early years. The work ethic came before ambition. The curiosity came before focus. The family routine came before academic discipline. I didn't invent these things in university. I just learned to scale them.

When people ask where my drive comes from, the answer is simple: home. My hometown. My family. The people who showed me that consistency beats intensity and that understanding matters more than appearance. I carry that with me every single day at UJ, and I will carry it beyond.

Where I came from shapes where I'm going.
